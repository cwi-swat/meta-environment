Character Classes

Enumerations of characters occur frequently in lexical definitions. They can be abbreviated by using character classes enclosed by `[' and `]'. A character class contains a list of zero or more characters (which stand for themselves) or character ranges such as, for instance, [0-9] as an abbreviation for the characters 0, 1, ..., 9. In a character range of the form - one of the following restrictions should apply:

  • and are both lower-case letters and follows in the alphabet, or
  • and are both upper-case letters and follows in the alphabet, or
  • and are both digits and the numeric value of is greater than that of , or
  • and are both escaped non-printable characters and the character code of is greater than that of .
